My Novatech Nagpur SDE Interview Experience

I'm excited to share my experience interviewing for an SDE role at Novatech Nagpur. The journey started with a technical interview on February 25th. On March 5th, I received an email informing me about the machine round scheduled for March 7th. This news made me a bit nervous, but I immediately began preparing by revising DSA (Data Structures and Algorithms), aptitude and reasoning, and software engineering concepts.

The Machine Round

On March 7th, The test was scheduled from 11 AM to 2 PM, and we arrived around 10 AM. Upon reaching the Novatech office, I had the opportunity to interact with some of the techies working there. I had a particularly good conversation with a brother named Vinay Chaitanya from Hyderabad, which helped ease my nerves.

The test itself consisted of several sections:

  • MCQs: There were 30 multiple-choice questions covering Java, OOPs (Object-Oriented Programming), and fundamental concepts. I felt confident about my answers in this section.
  • Coding Questions: Two coding questions were presented, ranging from medium to hard difficulty. I successfully solved one question and partially solved the second, passing some of the test cases.
  • Aptitude and Reasoning: This section went well, and I felt I performed adequately.
  • Software Engineering: This was the section where I struggled the most. As a third-year student, I hadn't yet covered software engineering in depth, which is typically a fourth-year subject. Despite my limited knowledge, I tried my best to answer the questions.

I completed the test around 1:30 PM, and after waiting for a while, around 6 PM, I received an email informing me that I hadn't cleared the software engineering theory section. It was disappointing, but I learned a valuable lesson about the importance of being prepared for all aspects of the interview process.

Key Takeaways

This experience highlighted the importance of comprehensive preparation, especially for software engineering concepts. It was a good learning experience, and I appreciate the opportunity to have interviewed with Novatech.

Even though I didn't get the job, I'm grateful for the experience and will continue to work hard to improve my skills. Thank you for reading!
